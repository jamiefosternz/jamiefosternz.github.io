Science in NCEA

Science is a versatile subject that leads to many opportunities later in life. Science can be defined as:

"The intellectual and practical activity encompassing the systematic study of the structure and behaviour of the physical and natural world through observation and experiment."

Broken down, this means that science consists of conducting experiments to find out more about our world. We utilise science in everyday applications, from cooking dinner to driving our cars. Because of this, society has a high demand for scientists which can create lucrative careers. Learn More

NCEA Junior Science

Junior Science in Year 9, 10, and 11 offers students a general overview of the 3 main fields of science while touching on more exotic subjects such as geology. In the majority of NZ schools, science is a compulsory subject. Junior science is usually 1 subject (usually about 1/6 of your time at school). This is a good balance between doing science and giving students the opportunity to do other subjects. Junior science covers Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, with Earth and Space science often being featured. Learn More


NCEA Senior Science

Senior Science in Year 12 and 13 allows students to specialize in fields of science they choose. Unlike Junior Science, specific fields (Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and Earth and Space) have full period times allocated to them. Having much more class time allows for a more in-depth understanding of science. Learn More
