Year 9 & 10

In the junior school, the science curriculum is set by individual schools. However, some resources are considered to be fairly standard. sciPAD is a great resource that covers a wide range of information across the curriculum.


Year 11

Year 11 is the first year of NCEA for most students, and this can be quite intimidating. Science in year 11 is still a general subject. Y11 Science differs to other subjects in that more emphasis is placed on external assessments. A common assessment structure consists of a small range of internals, and 3 externals, one for Biology, Chemistry, and Physics. the internals will likely consist of a written report and a practical physics investigation. The externals will likely be; Mechanics, Acids & Bases, and Genetic Variation.

Mechanics

Mechanics is a Physics-based external that judges a student's ability to calculate variable such as mass, force, and velocity based on known information. Mechanics is a fairly simple external as it is simply applied algebra - you are given all the formulas necessary (except for Fw=mg). It's up to you to choose and rearrange the formulas correctly.

Acids & Bases

Acids & Bases is slightly more complex, and requires you to memorise a fair amount of knowledge around chemical conventions and formulas. Part of the paper focuses around atom arrangements and ions, while the other focuses on acid-base reactions.

Genetic Variation

Genetic variation is the Biology-focused paper. It consists mostly of figuring out the genotype and phenotype of animals. This is done through the use of pedigree charts and punnet squares.
