Career pathways and opportunities

Science is necessary for an enormous variety of careers. It's important to note here that many of these careers require tertiary education. Your choice of university is an important decision that can affect the enjoyment and progress of your career. As such, it should be made having a good knowledge of what's to come.

Examples of Careers

As mentioned before, science is ingrained in many careers.

Physics is used in:

  • Engineering

  • Computer science & Software design

  • Teaching

  • Manufacturing

Chemistry is used in:

  • Materials design

  • Industrial processing

  • Analytic services

  • Medicine

Biology is used in:

  • Taxonomy

  • Ecology & Earth science

  • Bio-engineering

  • Medicine

Universities

New Zealand has eight universities, all of which are considered to be good. These are:

  • University of Auckland

  • AUT

  • University of Waikato

  • Massey University

  • Victoria University

  • University of Canterbury

  • Lincoln University

  • University of Otago

Of these, I will focus on University of Auckland, University of Canterbury, and University of Otago. While all these universities utilise all fields of science, they specialize in some specific areas. UoA offers a wide range of fairly "pure" research-based science degrees, such as biomedicine. UoC is physics-chemistry based, and has a heavy focus on applied sciences, such as engineering. Finally, UoO has a very specific focus on medicine.
